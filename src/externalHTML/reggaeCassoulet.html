Please Join Community Kitchens for Three Nights of

Reggae and Cassoulet

with

French Master Chef David Campigotto

at Kingston11, Uptown, Oakland

Friday, December 1st through Sunday, December 3

Apero at 5:30pm – Dinner at 7pm

Community Kitchens invites you to join us in welcoming French master chef David Campigotto to Oakland for three nights of food, wine and music. Chef David is the eponymous chef-owner of Chez David restaurant in Castelnaudary, France, where customers flock nightly to sample his traditionally prepared CASSOULET. Chef David is visiting Chicago and Oakland this winter where he will present his signature dish for a small number of lucky diners. This is Chef David’s first visit to Oakland, where is coming to show his support for Community Kitchens’ unique and important anti-hunger work in the East Bay. Proceeds from this event will fund Community Kitchens’ street meal program for 2024.

Chef David preparing the cassoulet

This is a rare opportunity to experience an authentic presentation of this classic Languedoc dish outside of southern France. Chef David is bringing traditional cassoulet tureens from his own restaurant and shipping in local AOC-designated Tarbais beans. Once landed in Oakland, he will begin the three-day process of preparing the cassoulet – grinding his own sausage, slow-cooking local Liberty duck, and, finally, braising the casserole for hours until it is capped with a thick, savory crust.

Each night, our guests will enjoy this southern French classic as part of a four-course meal with wine pairings prepared with the assistance of local chefs Chris Pastena (Calavera, Chop Bar), Nigel Jones (Kingston11, Calabash) and Kendall Jones (Luka’s Taproom, Hopscotch).

Dinner will be preceded each evening by a happy hour featuring apéritifs and snacks prepared by nightly guest chefs. Caribbean music and vibes provided by Kingston11. Read about Chef David’s previous cassoulet pop ups here!
